## 1. Gravity as Anchor

### 1.1 The reframing

In standard general relativity, gravity is described as the curvature of spacetime sourced by mass-energy through Einstein's field equations. It is treated as a "force" by analogy with the other interactions, even though Einstein's geometric formulation makes clear that gravity is not a force in the same sense. The standard treatment leaves the question "what *is* gravity, structurally?" unanswered.

Under UM, the answer is direct: **gravity is the boundary channel β, acting as a bidirectional constraint-anchor** between matter content and the light-channel rendering of bulk geometry.

The "anchor" reading is exact, not metaphorical. The four mechanical properties of a physical anchor:

1. **No energy added.** An anchor does not push; it constrains.
2. **Removes degrees of freedom.** A boat at anchor cannot drift; the anchor removes its translational DoF.
3. **Bidirectional.** Tension on the anchor is felt by both ends — the anchor pulls back as much as it is pulled.
4. **Information-carrying through tension.** Curvature-gradient information transports through the anchor's tension structure.

The four match general-relativistic gravity exactly:

1. Stress-energy is conserved (∇_μ T^μν = 0). Gravity does no work on a freely-falling body.
2. Geodesic constraint and event horizons. Mass curves spacetime, removing a freely-falling body's translational DoF from the matter-channel-only sector.
3. Stress-energy and curvature mutually couple (G_μν = (1/M_Pl²) T_μν). The bidirectionality is exact.
4. Curvature gradients propagate as gravitational waves at light speed. Information about source dynamics transports through the boundary channel.

The four-property correspondence is structural, not heuristic. Gravity *is* the boundary channel, viewed at macroscopic scale.

**(a) Spacetime is the boundary-channel substrate.** General relativity's "spacetime" is the macroscopic shape of β's stress-energy tension. When matter clumps, β tightens locally; this is what Einstein's field equations describe geometrically.

**(b) Equivalence principle is structural.** The equivalence of gravitational and inertial mass — the foundational principle of general relativity — is forced by the anchor reading: a boat at anchor cannot distinguish between being held by tension and being in free space, because the anchor's job is to remove distinguishability. The geodesic principle is the boat-at-anchor principle made geometric.

It is the constraint structure of the universe-spanning boundary channel. Linearised gravitational waves are coherent excitations of β; gravitons may emerge at the appropriate quantisation level, but the framework does not require them.

## 3. Black Holes as Strategy B Resolution

### 3.1 The two-strategy picture

The universe maintains coherent self-mapping by organising every coherent region around one dominant calibration source. We call this **Strategy A**: content organises around an anchor in stable equilibrium, with a recovery zone around the φ-fixed-point that absorbs ordinary perturbations.

When local conditions push the matter-channel state past the saddle at -1/φ — typically through over-density, runaway competing sources, or gravitational collapse exceeding the recovery threshold — Strategy A cannot hold. The universe's resolution is **Strategy B**: the region collapses out of light-channel participation entirely, becoming a pure-boundary object. The event horizon is the geometric surface at which this transition completes; the interior is in pure boundary-channel state, with zero light-channel overlap.

### 3.2 The black hole is not the error

Three structural points:

**(a) The unsustainable over-density that preceded it is the error condition; the black hole is the universe's resolution of that error.** A black hole is "an error by formation, not by existence." Once formed, the pure-boundary object is a stable anchor — its existence resolves rather than constitutes the failure of local mapping.

**(b) Strategy B produces the anchors that Strategy A organises around at the next scale up.** This is the universe's hierarchical self-organisation mechanism. The supermassive black hole at a galactic centre is a Strategy B output (early-galaxy core collapse) that becomes the boundary anchor around which the galaxy's Strategy A organisation is built. Stellar-mass black holes serve as anchors for binary or multi-body Strategy A configurations. Dark-matter halos are the universe's earliest and most foundational Strategy B residues — the first anchors, formed in the early universe.

**(c) Both strategies have stable basins and recovery zones; neither is failure-immune.** Strategy A regions can absorb perturbations within the recovery zone or, if the threshold is exceeded, transition to Strategy B. Strategy B objects can also be perturbed (Hawking radiation, accretion variability, halo mergers) and either re-stabilise or dissipate.

## 5. Mass as Light-Coupling-History-Dependent

A consequence of the channel structure that follows directly from the matter equation (Paper 1 eq. 16): **observable rest mass is a renormalised quantity dependent on the matter's light-coupling history.**

### 5.1 Light-coupled regime

For matter in continuous light-coupling regimes (terrestrial atoms, cosmic gas, anything whose history includes ongoing photon contact), the matter-channel dynamics are continuously sampled and renormalised through the light-channel back-reaction. The observable rest mass is the standard renormalised quantity.

### 5.2 Light-isolated regime

For matter in light-isolated regimes — boundary-only dark matter, black hole interiors, deep planetary cores — there is no continuous light-coupling history through which to renormalise. The matter-channel field has its own internal dynamics, but the observable mass we could attribute to it relative to our frame is structurally undefined or pinned to its last light-contact value.

### 5.3 Implications

**Dark-matter rest mass is structurally undefined.** Direct-detection searches assume DM has a well-defined rest mass that can in principle be measured. Under UM, **boundary-only matter does not have a well-defined rest mass relative to light-channel observers** because no light-coupling history exists from which to set the calibration. The question "what is the dark matter particle mass" is therefore not merely empirically unanswered but **structurally meaningless**. DM's gravitational effects are well-defined; its rest mass is not. The only available mass signature for DM is the boundary-channel readout (gravitational mass, lensing).

**Black hole interior rest mass is pinned to horizon-crossing value.** Subsequent matter-channel evolution inside the horizon does not produce externally-readable rest-mass changes. The ADM mass external observers attribute to a black hole is precisely the boundary-channel content of the enclosed matter; the matter-channel content of the interior is structurally inaccessible.

**Deep planetary cores are intermediate.** They are not light-isolated (deep cores have geological light-coupling history through past processes) but their current internal state is decoupled from external light fields. Their effective rest mass is the integrated light-coupling history of their constituent material, which is a stable but slowly-evolving quantity.
